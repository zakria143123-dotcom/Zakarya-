What is Passive Voice?

The passive voice is used when we want to emphasize the action or the receiver of the action rather than who performs it.

Formation

Active: Subject + verb + object
Passive: Object + be + past participle (+ by + agent)

Active: The chef cooks the meal.
Passive: The meal is cooked by the chef.

Passive in Different Tenses

Present Simple: am/is/are + past participle

‚Ä¢ English is spoken in many countries.
‚Ä¢ The office is cleaned every day.

Past Simple: was/were + past participle

‚Ä¢ The house was built in 1990.
‚Ä¢ The letters were sent yesterday.

Present Perfect: has/have been + past participle

‚Ä¢ The book has been read by millions.
‚Ä¢ The emails have been answered.

Future: will be + past participle

‚Ä¢ The project will be completed next week.
‚Ä¢ The results will be announced tomorrow.

When to Use Passive Voice

1. When the doer is unknown or unimportant

My car was stolen. (We don't know who stole it)

2. When we want to emphasize the action or result

The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci.

3. In formal or scientific writing

The experiment was conducted in a laboratory.
